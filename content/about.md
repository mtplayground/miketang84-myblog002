# Mike Tang

I write about building software that stays maintainable after the launch spike is over.

Most of my work sits at the boundary between product strategy and implementation detail: translating a vague idea into a system that can be shipped, observed, and improved without drama.

On this site you will find notes on engineering decisions, editorial workflows, and the tradeoffs that show up when a personal project starts behaving like a real product.

When I am not shipping, I am usually trimming complexity out of an existing stack, documenting the parts that tend to surprise future contributors, or turning half-finished experiments into something another person can actually run.
